Procedural Plant Propagation Project

The goal of this project is to practice the manipulation of meshes, and actors, in response to the lighting. Having them "Grow" if lit, and stagnate, eventually withering if they are unlit.

02/24/2021 The Flowers

Naturally you can't have a a plant that doesn't make babies, to make plant babys a lot of plants use flowers - lets try that.

To make the flower, I used a sphere and a cone as a place holder. The flower is circled in red, and its babys are circled in blue. Plants have maximum heights that are randomly assigned within a range at the start. When they make babys, the babies inherit the maximum height from their parents, with some deviation.

02/22/2021 The Light source

To start, I needed to see if light was hitting something; while my eyes can tell me whats lit, UNreal Engine was less cooperative. I settled on a solution revolving around rays.

On every tick, I cast 300 rays, centered on my light sources. If they strike an object, and that object is a plant, then they increase the amount of energy that plant has.

The rays are always being cast, even if they are not visible. While this may seem like a lot of separate interactions, rays casting is actually one of the "cheapest" calculations you can do in Unreal Engine, unless you have to visualize them all!
